Thick fog descends

Early Autumn

August 18 – August 22
← Back to Index

Dawn begins muffled in white. Fields, houses, and distant hills blur together as a heavy mist rolls in, smothering edges and muting sound.

This microseason is called 蒙霧升降 (Fukaki kiri matô), a reminder that even in late summer the world can feel hushed and uncertain.

Fog like this forms when warm, humid air meets cooler ground, letting tiny droplets hang suspended. Each morning becomes a slow unveiling as the sun burns through.

For a brief moment the landscape belongs to the clouds, inviting quiet walks and careful breaths before daylight sharpens the view again.

蒙霧升降

Morning fog settles
Fields whisper soft secrets
Sun waits to break through

– Original haiku
