Kirikodokoki

Lesser Heat

July 17 – July 21

As summer deepens, the air grows thicker and the hum of cicadas rises to a steady chorus. This microseason, Kirikodokoki, signals the approach of midsummer’s heavy heat.

In gardens, the first eggplants and cucumbers ripen, their coolness treasured against the humidity. Farmers observe the shifting sky and prepare for the height of the growing season.

Take time to savor the subtleties: the shimmering of distant heat haze, the morning’s dense dew, and the slow drift of clouds that promise fleeting rains.
