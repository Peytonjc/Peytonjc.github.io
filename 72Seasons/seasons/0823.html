Cotton flowers blossom

Decreasing Heat

August 23 – August 27
← Back to Index

As temperatures ease, fields of cotton begin to show their delicate white blooms, simple stars that hint at the fibers to come.

The season is called 綿柎開 (Wata no hana shibe hiraku), marking the opening of the bracts that cradle each flower.

Farmers watch the sky, hoping for dry days so the blossoms can mature without dampness. The plant’s downy fibers will later become thread, cloth, and comfort.

Standing in these fields, it is easy to imagine the summer heat being spun away, replaced by thoughts of softer, cooler days ahead.

綿柎開

Cotton blooms open
Late summer threads the light
Clouds weave autumn's cloth

– Original haiku
